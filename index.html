🏺 Origins and Mythologies

🏺

Ancient Egypt

Goddess Bastet

Revered as protectors of the home, cats were linked to the goddess Bastet. In ancient Egypt, they were considered sacred and divine.

🐾

Japan

Maneki-neko

The Maneki-neko brings good luck: raised paw and prosperity! This iconic waving cat statue is a symbol of fortune.

⚡

Northern Europe

Goddess Freya

In Norse mythology, the goddess Freya traveled in a chariot pulled by two giant cats called Bygul and Trjegul.

💡 Feline Curiosities

😴

Professional Sleepers

Cats sleep 12–16 hours a day, saving energy for short bursts of hunting.

😸

Therapeutic Purring

Purring vibrates at 20–50 Hz, frequencies that may promote bone healing.

🎯

Perfect Landing

With their righting reflex, cats land on their feet within 0.3 seconds.

🗣️

100 Vocalizations

Adult cats meow mainly to communicate with humans, not each other.
